Thank you for sponsoring Cal Hacks 2.0! On this page you will find a quick breakdown of your sponsorship benefits and any information you'll need for Cal Hacks 2.0 from October 9-11. We will also be hosting a dinner for all sponsors on Thursday, October 8th for which more information will be emailed out soon. This page will be updated as more information becomes available!

Attendees will start checking-in to Cal Hacks 2.0 at 6PM on Friday, October 9th and begin hacking immediately after the Opening Ceremony at 10PM. Hackers will submit their hacks by 10AM on Sunday, after which there will be a Science Fair style expo open to the public for all projects. Finally, we will be announcing the top 10 teams.

If you have any questions, you can contact me directly at karuna@calhacks.io or (510)640-2082. For details on your sponsorship benefits, click the headers below.

  • Unlimited Mentors and Engineers

    While the number of representatives you can bring from your company (including recruiters, mentors, engineers) is unlimited, we recommend bringing no more than five in an attempt to accomodate the hackers and respect venue capacity. Please enter the names and contact details of all of your representatives here.

  • Access to Sponsor Lounge

    The sponsor lounge will be a section reserved for your representatives to take a breather and relax. More details soon.

  • Sponsored API Prize

    You may also reward a certain hacker group or hack category such as Best Hardware Hack, Best Social Hack or hack that best utilizes your product. Hackers seem to be motivated more by prizes such as giant teddy bears, Lego Mindstorms kits, etc. over company coupons or credits.

  • API Demo

    After the opening ceremony, you will have 1-2 minutes to speak on stage and demonstrate your API, shoutout your company, or speak about your products.

  • Distribute Recruiting Materials

    Feel free to surprise our hackers with flyers or pamphlets describing job openings or what you're looking for in an engineer. More details coming soon.

  • Personal Table

    You will receive a table specifically for your company that can be decorated however you wish. You can place swag items, contact information, company banners, etc. The space is yours! It will remain for the entirety of the Hackathon and it does not have to be manned at all times. Dimensions and exact location coming soon.

  • On-Site Interviews

    Details for interviewing hackers coming soon.

  • Resume Book

    After the hackathon, we will link you to our database of resumes of all invited hackers. If there is a special reason you might need it beforehand, please let us know.

  • Sponsor a hacker "Den"

    Hacker Dens are areas of the venue that are laid out specifically for the sponsors to provide support on specific topics (iOS, web development, hardware, etc.). Hackers working or needing help on the topic you choose can then congregate at your den and find help from your engineers. Typically, the topic for the Den you sponsor would be a speciality of your company. If you would like to move your personal table into your Den, please let us know atleast two weeks beforehand and we'll be able to accomodate you.

  • Curated Recruiting Lunch/Dinner

    During the hackathon, our top sponsors can have one lunch or dinner with hackers they hand-pick and physically invite through special invitation cards we provide. More details will be provided later on.

  • Logo on Website & T-Shirt

    Check out your logo on the website here. You'll be able to check out the T-Shirt design soon!

  • Distribute Swag

    Please mail any company swag you'd like to distribute to [address]. Some good options for swag items include t-shirts (preferably for both genders), headbands, LED braclets, travel packs, shades, and especially socks!

  • Item in all Attendee Swag-bags

    You can include a swag item of your choice with your branding on it. Items can range from coupon codes to socks to pens.

  • Host a Workshop

    Workshops are 30-min long sessions for you to mentor hackers in your niche topics. If you have some topics that you would be interested in holding, please send them over, along with some preferred time slots of 30 minutes so we can fit that into our schedule as soon as possible. We'd be happy to help you brainstorm ideas as well. [EDIT for 1-hour SESSIONS]

  • Sponsored Mini-Event

    Examples of mini-events you could present would be a Code in the Dark challenge, or a lasertag game (as we had last year) to add some more excitement to the hackathon! Again, we'd be happy to help you brainstorm ideas.
